TokyoCleaner: The Automation Vision

From Manual Selection to Algorithmic Scheduling

IMPORTANT: Context

The scheduler staff has resigned. This document defines how we replace that role with a software-driven "Recommendation & Assignment Engine" to scale historical volume (6,400 orders/year) without increasing head-count.

01. Current "Mental Landscape"

Complexity currently lives in the scheduler's head. The departure creates architectural risks:

  • The Information Silo: Knowledge is limited to service periods. Transition risks are high if orders aren't "well-organized" during handover.
  • The Interruption Cycle: Phone/Chat adjustments (sick leave) disrupt primary workflow.
  • The Compliance Gap: Quality is currently binary: 0 complaints = Good; >0 = Bad.

02. Structural Requirements

A. Availability as an Entity

Moving from "checking calendars" to "querying data":

  • Recurring Patterns: Staff define baseline active windows.
  • Override Layer: Centralized sick leave requests submitted via app, instantly disqualifying availability without a manual phone call.

B. The Performance Record

Using historical data to replace human intuition:

  • Property Familiarity: Tracking repeat visits per property.
  • Complaint-Free Streak: Using binary complaint data as the primary quality filter.

03. The Scheduler Replacement Workflow

Step 1: Automated Rules

Instant SQL filtering for availability, pattern-fit, and active overrides.

Step 2: Algorithmic Ranking

Sorting candidates by Zone Priority (ML), Familiarity, and Workload Balance.

Step 3: One-Click Confirmation (Per-Order Workflow)

When you open an individual Booking Detail and click "Assign Staff", the system generates a unique recommended set. One click notifies staff and updates the customer portal.

04. Strategic ROI

Metric Manual (Scheduler) Automated (SaaS)
Assignment Speed 10–20 mins / booking < 1 second
Adjustments Disruptive Phone/Chat Seamless App Notification
Transport Costs Random / Sub-optimal Geographic Optimization
Scalability Limited by Headcount Unlimited (6,400+ orders)

We aren't just building a portal; we are building the "Brain" that manages the labor.
